Research

Research papers on technological change, labor markets, worker mobility, and skill development.

Technology and the Shifting Architecture of Occupational Skills

We develop a measurement framework from job postings in 2010 and 2018, constructing aligned skill spaces for 514 occupations covering over 75% of U.S. employment. Two geometric dimensions reveal three distinct processes: cognitive distance, scaffolded learning, and simplification. The qualitative nature of skill change determines worker outcomes, not magnitude alone.

November 2025 · Nikhil George, Christophe Combemale, Ramayya Krishnan, Rahul Telang

From Posting to Prediction: Building Validated Workforce Analytics

We develop a methodology using internal job postings and HCM data to measure employee skill fit. Validating against 1,034 internal hiring decisions, we find it robustly predicts hiring: applicants with higher skill fit are over twice as likely to be selected for the same role.

June 2025 · Nikhil George, Ramayya Krishnan, Rahul Telang

When TV Becomes a Stream: Content Decisions of a Video On Demand Service

We analyze how viewer heterogeneity in time preferences affects VOD content decisions, showing that streaming platforms can offer quality-differentiated content compared to linear TV.

May 2020 · Nikhil George, Siddhartha Sharma, Rahul Telang

Revisiting Discrepancies in Sanitation Statistics of Rural India

A response highlighting how the inclusion of census town data can alter observations about rural sanitation statistics in India.
